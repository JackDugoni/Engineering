Mechanical Engineering Cheat Sheet

Your quick reference for common engineering formulas and constants

Stress (σ)

σ = F / A

F: Applied force (N)

A: Cross-sectional area (m²)

Internal force per unit area under load.

Strain (ε)

ε = ΔL / L

ΔL: Change in length (m)

L: Original length (m)

Measure of deformation due to applied load.

Young's Modulus (E)

E = σ / ε

Stiffness of a material within its elastic limit.

Factor of Safety (FoS)

FoS = Ultimate Strength / Allowable Stress

Ultimate Strength: Maximum stress (Pa)

Allowable Stress: Design stress (Pa)

Design safety margin.

Bending Stress (σ)

σ = (M × c) / I

M: Bending moment (N·m)

c: Distance from neutral axis (m)

I: Moment of inertia (m⁴)

Stress distribution in a beam under bending load.

Equilibrium Conditions

ΣF = 0 and ΣM = 0

Conditions for static equilibrium in a structure.

Newton's Second Law

F = m a

m: Mass (kg)

a: Acceleration (m/s²)

Relates force, mass, and acceleration.

Uniform Acceleration

v = u + at
s = ut + ½at²
v² = u² + 2as

u: Initial velocity (m/s)

v: Final velocity (m/s)

a: Acceleration (m/s²)

s: Displacement (m)

t: Time (s)

Motion equations under constant acceleration.

Angular Kinematics

ω = ω₀ + αt
θ = ω₀t + ½αt²
ω² = ω₀² + 2αθ

ω: Angular velocity (rad/s)

ω₀: Initial angular velocity (rad/s)

α: Angular acceleration (rad/s²)

θ: Angular displacement (rad)

t: Time (s)

Describes rotational motion under constant acceleration.

Kinetic Energy

KE = ½ m v²

m: Mass (kg)

v: Velocity (m/s)

Energy possessed by a moving object.

Bernoulli's Equation

P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant

P: Pressure (Pa)

ρ: Fluid density (kg/m³)

v: Fluid velocity (m/s)

g: Acceleration due to gravity (m/s²)

h: Height (m)

Energy conservation in fluid flow.

Reynolds Number (Re)

Re = (ρ v L) / μ

ρ: Density (kg/m³)

v: Velocity (m/s)

L: Characteristic length (m)

μ: Dynamic viscosity (Pa·s)

Determines flow regime: laminar or turbulent.

Continuity Equation

A₁v₁ = A₂v₂

A: Cross-sectional area (m²)

v: Velocity (m/s)

Mass conservation in steady fluid flow.

Fourier's Law

Q = -kA (dT/dx)

Q: Heat transfer rate (W)

k: Thermal conductivity (W/m·K)

A: Cross-sectional area (m²)

dT/dx: Temperature gradient (K/m)

Describes conduction of heat through materials.

Newton's Law of Cooling

Q = hA (Tₛ - Tₑ)

h: Heat transfer coefficient (W/m²·K)

A: Surface area (m²)

Tₛ: Surface temperature (K)

Tₑ: Ambient temperature (K)

Describes convective heat transfer from surfaces.

Stefan-Boltzmann Law

Q = εσA (T⁴ - Tₛ⁴)

ε: Emissivity (dimensionless)

σ: 5.67×10⁻⁸ W/(m²·K⁴)

A: Surface area (m²)

T: Temperature (K)

Tₛ: Surrounding temperature (K)

Radiative heat transfer between surfaces.

Ideal Gas Law

PV = nRT

P: Pressure (Pa)

V: Volume (m³)

n: Moles (mol)

R: Gas constant (8.314 J/mol·K)

T: Temperature (K)

Relates state variables for an ideal gas.

First Law of Thermodynamics

ΔU = Q - W

ΔU: Change in internal energy (J)

Q: Heat added (J)

W: Work done by the system (J)

Energy conservation in thermodynamic processes.

Carnot Efficiency

η = 1 - (Tₙ / Tₕ)

Tₕ: Hot reservoir temperature (K)

Tₙ: Cold reservoir temperature (K)

Maximum efficiency of an ideal heat engine.

Gravitational Acceleration (g)

g = 9.81 m/s²

Acceleration due to gravity near Earth's surface.

Universal Gas Constant (R)

R = 8.314 J/(mol·K)

Relates energy scale to temperature for ideal gases.

Pi (π)

π ≈ 3.14159

Ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter.

Stefan-Boltzmann Constant (σ)

σ = 5.67×10⁻⁸ W/(m²·K⁴)

Used in blackbody radiation calculations.

Avogadro's Number (NA)

NA = 6.022×10²³ mol⁻¹

Number of particles in one mole of substance.

Boltzmann's Constant (kB)

kB = 1.38×10⁻²³ J/K

Relates the average kinetic energy of particles to temperature.

Speed of Light (c)

c = 2.998×10⁸ m/s

Speed of light in a vacuum.
