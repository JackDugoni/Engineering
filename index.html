Mechanical Engineering Cheat Sheet

Your quick reference for common engineering formulas

Stress (σ)

σ = F / A

F: Applied force (N)

A: Cross-sectional area (m²)

Explanation: Calculates the internal force per unit area within a material when subjected to external loads.

Strain (ε)

ε = ΔL / L

ΔL: Change in length (m)

L: Original length (m)

Explanation: A dimensionless measure of deformation representing how much a material stretches or compresses.

Young's Modulus (E)

E = σ / ε

Explanation: Relates stress to strain in the elastic region of a material, indicating its stiffness.

Torque (T)

T = F × r

F: Force (N)

r: Distance from the pivot (m)

Explanation: Measures the turning effect produced by a force acting at a distance from an axis.

Moment of Inertia (I)

I = (b h³) / 12

b: Base width (m)

h: Height (m)

Explanation: Quantifies a beam's resistance to bending, crucial for structural analysis.
