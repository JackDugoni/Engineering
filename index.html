Statics

Equilibrium Equations

Statics
∑F = 0, ∑M = 0
∑F Sum of all forces
∑M Sum of all moments

For a body to be in static equilibrium, the sum of all forces and moments acting on it must equal zero. This is the foundation of statics problems.

Moment of Force

Statics
M = r × F = r·F·sin(θ)
M Moment (N·m)
r Position vector (m)
F Force (N)
θ Angle between r and F

The moment of a force is a measure of its tendency to cause rotation about a point or axis, equal to the product of the force and the perpendicular distance to the point or axis.

Centroid of Common Shapes

Statics
Rectangle: x̄ = L/2, ȳ = H/2
x̄, ȳ Centroid coordinates
L Length
H Height

The centroid is the geometric center of a shape. For a rectangular shape, it is located at half the length and half the height from the origin.
