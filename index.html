Jack Dugoni's Engineering Cheat Sheet

Your quick reference for common engineering formulas and constants

Stress (σ)

σ = F / A

F: Applied force (N)

A: Cross-sectional area (m²)

Internal force per unit area under load.

Strain (ε)

ε = ΔL / L

ΔL: Change in length (m)

L: Original length (m)

Measure of deformation due to applied load.

Young's Modulus (E)

E = σ / ε

Stiffness of a material within its elastic limit.

Factor of Safety (FoS)

FoS = Ultimate Strength / Allowable Stress

Ultimate Strength: Maximum stress (Pa)

Allowable Stress: Design stress (Pa)

Design safety margin.

Bending Stress (σ)

σ = (M × c) / I

M: Bending moment (N·m)

c: Distance from neutral axis (m)

I: Moment of inertia (m⁴)

Stress distribution in a beam under bending load.

Equilibrium Conditions

ΣF = 0 and ΣM = 0

Conditions for static equilibrium in a structure.

Equilibrium Conditions

ΣF = 0, ΣM = 0

For a body in static equilibrium, the sum of forces and moments must be zero.

Force Components

F_x = F cosθ, F_y = F sinθ

F: Force magnitude (N)

θ: Angle with respect to x-axis (degrees)

Breaks a force into x and y components.

Moment of a Force

M = F × d

F: Force (N)

d: Perpendicular distance from pivot (m)

Measures rotational effect of a force.

Couple Moment

M = F × r

r: Distance between force pairs (m)

Describes rotational force of two equal and opposite forces.

Centroid of a Rectangle

x̄ = b/2, ȳ = h/2

b: Base width (m)

h: Height (m)

Determines the center of mass of a rectangular shape.

Centroid of a Triangle

x̄ = b/3, ȳ = h/3

Location of centroid for a right-angled triangle.

Parallel Axis Theorem

I = I_c + A d²

I_c: Moment of inertia at centroid

A: Area (m²)

d: Distance from centroid (m)

Used to determine moment of inertia about any axis.

Perpendicular Axis Theorem

I_z = I_x + I_y

Used for thin, flat bodies to find moment of inertia.

Shear Force (V)

V = ΣF_y

Sum of vertical forces acting on a beam section.

Bending Moment (M)

M = Σ(F × d)

Measures the bending effect of forces on a beam.

Shear Stress (τ)

τ = V / A

Stress due to shear force in a material.

Maximum Bending Stress

σ = (M c) / I

Stress in a beam due to bending.

Section Modulus

S = I / c

Determines bending strength of a cross-section.

Moment of Inertia of a Circle

I = (π d⁴) / 64

Resistance of a circular section to bending.

Moment of Inertia of a Hollow Circle

I = (π (D⁴ - d⁴)) / 64

Resistance of a hollow circular section to bending.

Method of Joints

ΣF_x = 0, ΣF_y = 0

Used to solve for forces in truss members.

Method of Sections

ΣF = 0, ΣM = 0

Alternative approach for solving trusses.

Friction Force

F_f = μN

Force resisting motion due to contact surfaces.

Angle of Repose

tanθ = μ

Angle at which an object starts sliding on a surface.

Work Done by a Force

W = F d cosθ

Work done when a force moves an object.

Newton's Second Law

F = m a

m: Mass (kg)

a: Acceleration (m/s²)

Relates force, mass, and acceleration.

Uniform Acceleration

v = u + at
s = ut + ½at²
v² = u² + 2as

u: Initial velocity (m/s)

v: Final velocity (m/s)

a: Acceleration (m/s²)

s: Displacement (m)

t: Time (s)

Motion equations under constant acceleration.

Angular Kinematics

ω = ω₀ + αt
θ = ω₀t + ½αt²
ω² = ω₀² + 2αθ

ω: Angular velocity (rad/s)

ω₀: Initial angular velocity (rad/s)

α: Angular acceleration (rad/s²)

θ: Angular displacement (rad)

t: Time (s)

Describes rotational motion under constant acceleration.

Kinetic Energy

KE = ½ m v²

m: Mass (kg)

v: Velocity (m/s)

Energy possessed by a moving object.

First Equation of Motion

v = u + at

u: Initial velocity (m/s)

v: Final velocity (m/s)

a: Acceleration (m/s²)

t: Time (s)

Relates velocity, acceleration, and time for uniformly accelerated motion.

Second Equation of Motion

s = ut + ½at²

u: Initial velocity (m/s)

s: Displacement (m)

a: Acceleration (m/s²)

t: Time (s)

Calculates displacement for uniformly accelerated motion.

Third Equation of Motion

v² = u² + 2as

u: Initial velocity (m/s)

v: Final velocity (m/s)

a: Acceleration (m/s²)

s: Displacement (m)

Relates velocity, acceleration, and displacement without time.

Average Velocity

v_avg = (u + v) / 2

u: Initial velocity (m/s)

v: Final velocity (m/s)

Calculates average velocity for constant acceleration.

Displacement using Average Velocity

s = v_avg × t

v_avg: Average velocity (m/s)

s: Displacement (m)

t: Time (s)

Alternative way to calculate displacement using average velocity.

First Equation of Angular Motion

ω = ω₀ + αt

ω₀: Initial angular velocity (rad/s)

ω: Final angular velocity (rad/s)

α: Angular acceleration (rad/s²)

t: Time (s)

Angular equivalent of linear velocity equation.

Second Equation of Angular Motion

θ = ω₀t + ½αt²

ω₀: Initial angular velocity (rad/s)

θ: Angular displacement (rad)

α: Angular acceleration (rad/s²)

t: Time (s)

Calculates angular displacement for rotational motion.

Third Equation of Angular Motion

ω² = ω₀² + 2αθ

ω₀: Initial angular velocity (rad/s)

ω: Final angular velocity (rad/s)

α: Angular acceleration (rad/s²)

θ: Angular displacement (rad)

Relates angular velocity, acceleration, and displacement.

Angular Displacement using Average Angular Velocity

θ = ω_avg × t

ω_avg: Average angular velocity (rad/s)

θ: Angular displacement (rad)

t: Time (s)

Alternative method for calculating angular displacement.

Average Angular Velocity

ω_avg = (ω₀ + ω) / 2

ω₀: Initial angular velocity (rad/s)

ω: Final angular velocity (rad/s)

Calculates average angular velocity for constant angular acceleration.

Bernoulli's Equation

P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant

P: Pressure (Pa)

ρ: Fluid density (kg/m³)

v: Fluid velocity (m/s)

g: Acceleration due to gravity (m/s²)

h: Height (m)

Energy conservation in fluid flow.

Reynolds Number (Re)

Re = (ρ v L) / μ

ρ: Density (kg/m³)

v: Velocity (m/s)

L: Characteristic length (m)

μ: Dynamic viscosity (Pa·s)

Determines flow regime: laminar or turbulent.

Continuity Equation

A₁v₁ = A₂v₂

A: Cross-sectional area (m²)

v: Velocity (m/s)

Mass conservation in steady fluid flow.

Fourier's Law

Q = -kA (dT/dx)

Q: Heat transfer rate (W)

k: Thermal conductivity (W/m·K)

A: Cross-sectional area (m²)

dT/dx: Temperature gradient (K/m)

Describes conduction of heat through materials.

Newton's Law of Cooling

Q = hA (Tₛ - Tₑ)

h: Heat transfer coefficient (W/m²·K)

A: Surface area (m²)

Tₛ: Surface temperature (K)

Tₑ: Ambient temperature (K)

Describes convective heat transfer from surfaces.

Stefan-Boltzmann Law

Q = εσA (T⁴ - Tₛ⁴)

ε: Emissivity (dimensionless)

σ: 5.67×10⁻⁸ W/(m²·K⁴)

A: Surface area (m²)

T: Temperature (K)

Tₛ: Surrounding temperature (K)

Radiative heat transfer between surfaces.

Ideal Gas Law

PV = nRT

P: Pressure (Pa)

V: Volume (m³)

n: Moles (mol)

R: Gas constant (8.314 J/mol·K)

T: Temperature (K)

Relates state variables for an ideal gas.

First Law of Thermodynamics

ΔU = Q - W

ΔU: Change in internal energy (J)

Q: Heat added (J)

W: Work done by the system (J)

Energy conservation in thermodynamic processes.

Carnot Efficiency

η = 1 - (Tₙ / Tₕ)

Tₕ: Hot reservoir temperature (K)

Tₙ: Cold reservoir temperature (K)

Maximum efficiency of an ideal heat engine.
