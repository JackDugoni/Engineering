Equilibrium of Forces

For a body to be in static equilibrium, the sum of all forces and moments must be zero.

∑F = 0, ∑M = 0

∑F Sum of all forces

∑M Sum of all moments

Moment of Force

The moment of a force is a measure of its tendency to cause a body to rotate about a specific point.

M = r × F

M Moment (N·m)

r Lever arm (m)

F Force (N)

× Cross product

Newton's Second Law

The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass.

F = ma

F Force (N)

m Mass (kg)

a Acceleration (m/s²)

Work-Energy Principle

The change in kinetic energy of an object is equal to the net work done on the object.

W = ΔKE = ½m(v₂² - v₁²)

W Work (J)

ΔKE Change in kinetic energy (J)

m Mass (kg)

v₁ Initial velocity (m/s)

v₂ Final velocity (m/s)

Bernoulli's Equation

An increase in the speed of a fluid occurs simultaneously with a decrease in pressure or a decrease in the fluid's potential energy.

P₁ + ½ρv₁² + ρgh₁ = P₂ + ½ρv₂² + ρgh₂

P Pressure (Pa)

ρ Fluid density (kg/m³)

v Fluid velocity (m/s)

g Gravitational acceleration (m/s²)

h Height (m)

Reynolds Number

A dimensionless quantity used to predict whether fluid flow will be laminar or turbulent.

Re = ρvD/μ

Re Reynolds number

ρ Fluid density (kg/m³)

v Fluid velocity (m/s)

D Characteristic length (m)

μ Dynamic viscosity (Pa·s)

First Law of Thermodynamics

Energy can neither be created nor destroyed, only transferred or converted from one form to another.

ΔU = Q - W

ΔU Change in internal energy (J)

Q Heat added to the system (J)

W Work done by the system (J)

Thermal Efficiency

The ratio of useful work output to the heat input in a heat engine.

η = W/Q₁ = 1 - Q₂/Q₁

η Thermal efficiency

W Work output (J)

Q₁ Heat input (J)

Q₂ Heat rejected (J)

Stress

The internal resistance of an object to a deforming force applied to it.

σ = F/A

σ Stress (Pa)

F Applied force (N)

A Cross-sectional area (m²)

Strain

The measure of deformation representing the displacement between particles in a body relative to a reference length.

ε = ΔL/L₀

ε Strain

ΔL Change in length (m)

L₀ Original length (m)

Fourier's Law of Heat Conduction

The rate of heat transfer through a material is proportional to the negative gradient in temperature and to the area perpendicular to that gradient.

q = -k∇T

q Heat flux (W/m²)

k Thermal conductivity (W/m·K)

∇T Temperature gradient (K/m)

Newton's Law of Cooling

The rate of heat loss of a body is directly proportional to the difference in temperatures between the body and its surroundings.

q = hA(T_s - T_∞)

q Heat transfer rate (W)

h Convective heat transfer coefficient (W/m²·K)

A Surface area (m²)

T_s Surface temperature (K)

T_∞ Fluid temperature (K)

Gear Ratio

The ratio between the angular velocity of the input gear to the angular velocity of the output gear.

GR = ω₁/ω₂ = N₂/N₁

GR Gear ratio

ω₁ Angular velocity of input gear (rad/s)

ω₂ Angular velocity of output gear (rad/s)

N₁ Number of teeth on input gear

N₂ Number of teeth on output gear

Shaft Torsion

The relationship between applied torque and the resulting angle of twist in a shaft.

τ = Tr/J

τ Shear stress (Pa)

T Torque (N·m)

r Radius of shaft (m)

J Polar moment of inertia (m⁴)
